What it is

Two programs.
One coordinated care team.

Most patients qualify for both. Together, they give your provider a continuous picture of your health — and give you a real person to call when something doesn't feel right.

Remote Patient Monitoring

RPM — devices that watch the trends

Connected devices ship to your home and send daily readings to your care team automatically. No spreadsheets, no apps to fight with — just numbers flowing in the background so we can spot changes early.

  • Glucose, blood pressure, and weight tracked from home
  • Care team reviews readings and flags anything off
  • Alerts sent to you and your provider in real time
  • Covered by Medicare and most major insurance
Chronic Care Management

CCM — a coach who knows your story

A dedicated health coach checks in monthly to review your readings, your medications, and how you're actually feeling. Between visits with your doctor, they're the person making sure nothing falls through the cracks.

  • Monthly 1-on-1 check-ins with a real coach
  • Help with medications, refills, and side effects
  • Coordination with specialists, labs, and pharmacy
  • Message your coach anytime through the app
Real progress happens between visits. Your plan keeps you connected, monitored, and supported every day.

Common questions

Common questions, clear answers.

Honest answers about how the program works, what's covered, and what to expect once you're enrolled. Have a question we missed? Ask your coach on the welcome call.

What's the difference between RPM and CCM?
Remote Patient Monitoring (RPM) is the device side — connected glucose monitors, blood pressure cuffs, and scales that send daily readings to your care team. Chronic Care Management (CCM) is the human side — monthly 1-on-1 calls with a coach who reviews your numbers, your medications, and your overall plan. Most patients are enrolled in both.
Is this covered by insurance?
We accept most major insurance plans, including Medicare. After you fill out the form, our team verifies your benefits and walks you through any out-of-pocket costs before anything ships. Most Medicare patients pay $0 out of pocket.
Which conditions does the program cover?
Diabetes (Type 1 & Type 2), hypertension, obesity, congestive heart failure, COPD, asthma, kidney disease, and more. If you have two or more chronic conditions expected to last at least 12 months, you likely qualify.
Which CGM will I use — Dexcom or Freestyle Libre 3?
Your provider chooses the device that's the best fit for you. Both pair seamlessly with our care team and give continuous, fingerstick-free monitoring.
How does the care team actually help?
Health coaches review your trends, send check-ins, and flag anything concerning to your provider. You'll have someone to call when a number looks off — not a chatbot.
How fast does it ship?
After your confirmation call and benefits check, your CGM is sent to your home. Most patients are monitoring within a week.
Do I have to download an app?
The CGM uses its own free app for readings, but you don't need any technical skill. We walk you through setup step-by-step on your confirmation call.
